\insert{What is the difference between}{The first of these two forms is a
\term{general warranty deed}, in which the grantor provides guarantees, called
\term[covenant of title]{covenants of title}, to the grantee as to ownership of
the conveyed land. The second form is a \term{quitclaim deed}, in which the
grantor provides no covenants. }

\item A third type of deed, the \term{special warranty deed}, is allowed in many
jurisdictions. This deed protects the grantee against title defects like a
general warranty deed, but only against defects arising from the actions or
omissions of the grantor.
